How to Make the Crust

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups Crisco (vegetable shortening)
  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 whole egg
  • 5 tbsp. cold water
  • 1 tbsp. white vinegar
  • 1 tsp. salt

Directions

  1. Slowly mix the Flour with the vegetable shortening in a large bowl
  2. In a smaller bowl, beat the egg with a fork
  3. Add the egg to the larger bowl
  4. Add the cold water, white vinegar, and salt then mix until it becomes a dough
  5. Seperate the dough into thirds or halves depending on how thick you want your crust
  6. Please note that you could also buy pre-made crust for your pie

How to Make the Purée

Ingredients

  • 2 small pumpkins

Directions

  1. Cut the small pumpkins in half
  2. Scoop out as much pulp and seeds as possible
  3. Place the small pumpkins on a tray
  4. Bake at 350°F for about 45 minutes
  5. Once golden brown, peel away the skin from the pumpkins
  6. Lastly, put chunks of the mush into a food processor or mash them up with a potato masher until purée is smooth

How to Make the Pie Filling

Ingredients

  • 15-oz. pumpkin purée
  • 1 1/4 c. heavy whipping cream
  • 1/2 c. granulated sugar
  • 1/4 c. packed light brown sugar
  • 2 tsp. pumpkin pie spice
  • 1 tsp. vanilla extract
  • 1/2 tsp. kosher salt
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 large egg yolk

Directions

  1. Add the purée, cream, and sugars into a bowl and whisk together
  2. Whisk in the rest until the mix is smooth

How to Make Whipped Cream

Ingredients

  • 1 c. heavy whipping cream
  • 3 tbsp. sugar
  • 1 tsp. vanilla extract

Directions

  1. Add all of the ingredients into a bowl
  2. Whisk by hand or with a hand mixer until the cream in thick and stands up on its own

How to Make the Pie

Ingredients

  • a 9" pie plate
  • pie crust
  • pie filling
  • whipped cream

Directions

  1. Start with the pie crust
  2. Roll it out on a lightly floured surface to be about 13" round
  3. Then transfer into a 9" pie plate
  4. Tuck the edges of the crust to be even with the pie plate
  5. Crimp the edges
  6. Prick the crust all over with a fork
  7. Put crust in fridge for an hour
  8. Preheat the oven to 400˚F
  9. Once the crust is cooled, take out o fthe fridge
  10. line the crust with parchment paper
  11. Fill the pie to the top with baking weights or dried beans
  12. Bake in the oven for 13 to 15 minutes, until the edges look dry and have a bit of color on them
  13. Remove the weights and parchment paper
  14. Place back into the oven for another 4 to 6 minutes until the bottom looks dry and colored too
  15. Turn the temperature down to 350°F
  16. Add the pie filling
  17. Bake for 55 to 60 minutes
  18. Let it cool completely
  19. Serve with whipped cream
